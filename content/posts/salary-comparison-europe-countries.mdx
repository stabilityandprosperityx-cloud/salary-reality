## How to read this comparison honestly

- **Gross** is what employers usually advertise for employees.
- **Net** depends on family status, deductions, and local rules.
- **Tax pressure** here means a coarse “high / medium / lower” label for typical employees, not a precise percentage for every person.
- **Rent** is a rough one-bedroom central-city planning anchor, not a guarantee.

## Cost of living traps that distort comparisons

- **Short-term Airbnb** pricing is not long-term rent.
- **International schools** can dwarf tax differences for families.
- **Car ownership** is expensive in some cities and unnecessary in others.
- **Healthcare** costs differ sharply between systems.

## FAQ

### Which European country pays software engineers the most?

Switzerland and the US-remote-paid EU expat lifestyle often top cash outcomes, but rent and currency matter. There is no single winner for every person.

### Is Poland still a budget destination for remote workers?

Poland can offer strong value, but Warsaw and Kraków rents have risen; model your own housing tier.

### Why does net salary vary so much at the same gross?

Social contributions, income tax brackets, and personal deductions differ. Marital status and children can move net dramatically.

### Should I move for a higher gross?

Only if **net savings after expenses** increases. A higher gross in London can still mean lower savings than a moderate gross in Lisbon.
